# Offer and Pricing for Solana Products

Use this when the user asks how to package, price, monetize, or sell a Solana product, service, API, protocol, game, or devtool.

## Core Principle

Price the outcome and the adoption path, not the chain, token, or engineering effort.

Solana builders often underprice because the product is early, open-source, or hackathon-born. They also overreach by trying to monetize with a token before proving repeated utility. Start with the smallest paid or committed unit of value.

## Pick the Business Model by Product Type

| Product Type | Common First Offer | Monetization Pattern |
|--------------|--------------------|----------------------|
| Developer tool, SDK, CLI | Setup sprint, hosted version, support plan | Free OSS plus paid cloud, support, or team features |
| RPC, indexing, webhooks, data API | Paid pilot with usage cap | Usage-based, tiered SaaS, enterprise SLA |
| Wallet or consumer UX tool | Design partner pilot with wallet team | B2B licensing, rev share, sponsorship, or embedded fee |
| DeFi protocol | Liquidity, integration, or market-maker partnership | Protocol fees, partner revenue, grants, incentive programs |
| Security or monitoring | Risk diagnostic or continuous monitoring pilot | Retainer, per-protocol plan, incident response add-on |
| Game or consumer app | Founder cohort, season pass, NFT/token-gated utility only if useful | IAP, subscription, marketplace fee, sponsorship |
| DAO or ops tooling | Paid setup and workflow migration | Seat-based SaaS, treasury ops retainer |
| AI agent for Solana workflows | Manual-first concierge pilot | Subscription plus usage, done-with-you setup, API |
| Education or community product | Cohort, workshop, or applied sprint | Cohort fee, sponsorship, certification, membership |

If the product is too early for price, ask for a commitment that still creates signal: data access, public quote, integration time, design partner name, intro, or weekly usage.

## Minimum Viable Offer

Use this for first paid pilots:

```text
In [timeframe], we help [ICP] achieve [specific Solana-relevant outcome] through [mechanism].
It includes [2 to 3 deliverables].
Success metric: [observable result].
Pilot commitment: [$X or non-cash commitment].
```

Examples:

```text
In 10 days, we help Solana wallet teams identify the top 5 causes of failed first-time swap attempts through transaction replay analysis and support-ticket clustering. It includes a dashboard, failure taxonomy, and 3 UX fixes. Pilot: $2,500 or public design-partner quote plus anonymized data access.
```

```text
In 7 days, we help hackathon teams integrate a Solana payment flow using a tested template and support channel. It includes setup, example code, and one review. Pilot: 5 teams free in exchange for recorded setup times and public feedback.
```

## Pricing Methods

### 1. Paid Pilot Floor

Use for B2B tools, infra, analytics, security, or workflow products.

Set a price that is:

- High enough to prove seriousness
- Low enough for a small team to approve quickly
- Narrow enough to deliver manually

Suggested ranges to reason from, not blindly copy:

- $500 to $2,500: early founder-led diagnostic or setup sprint
- $2,500 to $10,000: B2B pilot with custom analysis, integration, or monitoring
- $10,000+: enterprise, security, infra, or protocol-critical work with high downside risk

Always tie the number to value:

```text
If this saves 20 support hours per month, prevents one failed integration, or improves conversion for a high-value flow, what is that worth?
```

### 2. Open-Source Plus Paid Support

Use when distribution depends on trust and adoption.

Free layer:

- Core library, template, CLI, or local tool
- Docs and quickstart
- Example integrations

Paid layer:

- Hosted service
- Team dashboard
- SLA or priority support
- Private integrations
- Compliance or security reporting
- Custom monitoring

Do not hide all value behind a paywall if developer adoption is the wedge.

### 3. Usage-Based SaaS

Use for APIs, indexing, monitoring, RPC, webhook, analytics, or automation products.

Define:

- Unit of usage: requests, webhooks, wallets tracked, transactions decoded, projects, seats, alerts, GB, or active users
- Free threshold: enough to reach first value
- Paid threshold: starts before costs become painful
- Overages: simple and predictable

Avoid complicated token-denominated pricing for first versions unless the buyer already budgets that way.

### 4. Integration or Partnership Offer

Use when the product needs ecosystem credibility.

Offer:

```text
We will integrate with [protocol/wallet/tool] in [timeframe], produce [public demo/docs], and deliver [specific benefit] to your users. In return we ask for [distribution, design partner quote, grant, co-marketing, or paid support].
```

This works best when the partner receives a direct user outcome, not just ecosystem goodwill.

### 5. Grant-Funded Milestones

Use when the buyer is an ecosystem program, foundation, DAO, or grant committee.

Milestones should be user-facing:

- Public repo and docs
- Working demo
- 3 external integrations
- 5 user interviews with published synthesis
- 10 developers completing a dry-run
- Security review checklist
- Usage dashboard

Avoid milestones that are only "finish backend" or "launch token."

## Pricing Anti-Patterns

Push back when the user says:

- "We will monetize with a token later"
- "It should be free because it is for the ecosystem"
- "We can charge after we get users"
- "No one will pay for developer tools"
- "The grant is the business model"
- "We need volume before pricing"

Respond:

```text
What is the smallest commitment a serious user would make this week if the pain is real?
```

## Offer Output

When asked for packaging or pricing, return:

```text
Offer name:

ICP:

Pain trigger:

Promise:

Mechanism:

Deliverables:

Timeframe:

Success metric:

Price or commitment:

Why this price is defensible:

Risk reversal:

What is not included:

Next sales action:
```

## Scope Guard

Never promise legal compliance, security safety, token appreciation, guaranteed yields, guaranteed grants, guaranteed exchange listings, or investor outcomes.

When relevant, say:

```text
This is a go-to-market recommendation, not legal, financial, or security advice. Route those pieces to the appropriate specialist before launch.
```
